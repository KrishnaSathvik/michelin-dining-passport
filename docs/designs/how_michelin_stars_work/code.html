Understanding the distinctions

How Michelin Stars Work

Originating in 1900 as a guide for motorists, the Michelin Guide has evolved into the most recognized culinary distinction globally. Anonymous inspectors assess restaurants purely on the quality of the food on the plate, evaluating quality of ingredients, harmony of flavors, mastery of techniques, personality of the chef, and consistency across visits.

One Star

"A very good restaurant in its category"

Signifies a restaurant that consistently prepares food to a high standard, using high-quality ingredients. A single star is a mark of exceptional talent and execution, indicating a kitchen operating at a level well above average.

Tracked count: 216
**

Two Stars

"Excellent cooking, worth a detour"

Awarded when the personality and talent of the chef are evident in expertly crafted dishes. The food is refined and inspired, marking a destination that elevates dining from a meal to a memorable experience.

Tracked count: 39
***

Three Stars

"Exceptional cuisine, worth a special journey"

The ultimate accolade, awarded for the superlative execution of culinary art. These restaurants offer a peerless dining experience where ingredients are exemplary, techniques are flawless, and every dish is a masterpiece.

Tracked count: 16

Beyond the Stars

Bib Gourmand

Recognizes establishments that deliver high-quality food at moderate prices. While Dining Passport focuses primarily on starred venues, select highly-curated Bib Gourmand selections appear in specific city guides.

Michelin Green Star

Highlights restaurants at the forefront of sustainable gastronomy. Dining Passport tags these venues prominently within our curations, supporting ethical dining practices.
